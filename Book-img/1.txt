3. Harry Potter and the Prisoner of Azkaban (1999)
For his third year, Harry returns to Hogwarts to find that the wizarding prison, Azkaban, has had an inmate escape. The escaped prisoner, Sirius Black, is a dangerous murderer who is believed to be hunting Harry. The school is protected by Dementors, soul-sucking creatures from Azkaban, but Harry must discover the truth behind Black's escape
4. The Casual Vacancy (2012)
Rowling's first novel for adults, The Casual Vacancy, is a social satire set in the fictional English town of Pagford. After the unexpected death of a beloved town figure, a vacancy opens on the parish council, setting off a conflict among the residents. The novel delves into the dark secrets and unspoken tensions that simmer beneath the town's placid surface. 
5. The Ink Black Heart (2022)
Published under the pseudonym Robert Galbraith, this is the sixth book in the Cormoran Strike detective series. The story follows private detectives Cormoran Strike and Robin Ellacott as they investigate the murder of Edie Ledwell, co-creator of a popular online cartoon, who was harassed by a mysterious online figure. The case pulls them into the dark world of online anonymity and cyberbullying
Plot Overview
The story begins with Harry Potter experiencing a troubling dream about Voldemort and a murder. He soon attends the Quidditch World Cup with the Weasleys, where Death Eaters attack, signaling Voldemort's growing power. Upon returning to Hogwarts, it is announced that the school will host the Triwizard Tournament, a magical competition between three schools: Hogwarts, Beauxbatons, and Durmstrang.
Despite being underage and not entering his name, Harry is mysteriously chosen as a fourth champion alongside Cedric Diggory, Fleur Delacour, and Viktor Krum. Throughout the tournament, Harry faces three perilous tasks: battling a dragon, rescuing friends from mermaids, and navigating a treacherous maze. Each task tests his courage, intelligence, and magical abilities.
